Used traditionally in Chinese medicine, Astragalus is recognized for its anti-inflammatory, anti-aging, and immune-boosting properties. Some studies suggest it can improve sugar metabolism in people with type 2 diabetes, contributing to lower glucose levels.

African Mango

African Traditionally used in African medicine, the African Mango is known for its digestive health benefits. The plant's roots, bark, and leaves are thought to aid in digestion, reduce triglycerides and cholesterol, and potentially support weight loss.

Guarana

A berry from the Amazon, Guarana is similar in size to coffee beans and is rich in antioxidants. It's often used in energy drinks for its stimulating properties, including theobromine, theophylline, and caffeine, and might support metabolism and weight loss.

Maca Root

Maca Native to the Andes, Maca Root is used for its medicinal properties. It's believed to improve insulin sensitivity, potentially reducing blood sugar levels and preventing metabolic diseases. Its effectiveness might be attributed to its high antioxidant and anti-inflammatory content.
8

Ginseng

Ginseng is a well-known herb used for its therapeutic properties, including enhancing energy levels and immune function. It's also studied for its potential to improve blood sugar control and increase insulin sensitivity in individuals with diabetes.

Berberine

Berberine is a compound found in several plants, used for its numerous health benefits. It's particularly noted for its effectiveness in lowering blood sugar levels, improving cholesterol levels, and helping in the management of diabetes.
10

Bitter Melon

Bitter Bitter Melon is a tropical vine that bears a fruit with a distinct bitter taste, used traditionally in various Asian cuisines and medicinal practices. It's known for its ability to lower blood sugar levels and is often used as a natural remedy for diabetes.
